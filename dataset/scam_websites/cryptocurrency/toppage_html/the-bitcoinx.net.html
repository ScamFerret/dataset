Bitcoin or Altcoins?

For many new cryptocurrency investors, the first decision is to decide whether to invest in Bitcoin or altcoins.

Bitcoin is the first-ever cryptocurrency, and it has been very successful and continues to dominate the space. However, its price has literally exploded and the price of 1 Bitcoin is out of reach for many investors. On the other hand, altcoins are alternative cryptocurrencies to Bitcoin. Most altcoins seek to improve upon the deficiencies of Bitcoin or any other major cryptocurrencies.

There are numerous altcoins, and they differ in utility from security tokens and platform coins to ‘meme coins’ and governance tokens. Bitcoin started with a price of less than $1, and it has already printed a high of around $70k. These are the kind of returns that altcoins seek to replicate.

There have been quite a few successes, but most altcoins have failed to take off. The major appeal for altcoins is usually their low prices which imply a high ceiling for investor returns. For instance, if Bitcoin rises from 20k to 40k, an investor will have earned 3x his investment; whereas, if an altcoin rises from $0.001 to just $0.01, it would represent a 10x return. But this high reward comes with high risk. Altcoins are very illiquid and enjoy low popularity. It is easy to get stuck with your coins, or transaction costs may be so huge that you won’t get to enjoy your true profits.

On the other hand, Bitcoin has high liquidity and is backed by a huge community that have accepted it as the premium digital store of value.

Can Governments Destroy Bitcoin?

Can Governments Destroy Bitcoin?

"One of the major headwinds that Bitcoin has faced in its journey is government regulation. But can the government really kill Bitcoin?

As a decentralized digital currency, Bitcoin is largely outside the crosshairs of the government. But a government has the ability to ban or at least limit its usage. For instance, a government can enact a law that bans the use of Bitcoin as a currency within its jurisdictions. Banks and other financial institutions can also be cautioned against accepting Bitcoin as a mode of payment. But this ban cannot prevent two parties from reaching an agreement about using Bitcoin as a means of exchange.

If a ban is ineffective, governments can also decide to take the bull by its horns and launch alternatives for Bitcoin, or rather competitors. This would involve leveraging blockchain technologies to launch central bank digital currencies or similar alternatives that will promote transparency and enhance security in payments. This can be done with the purpose of limiting the motivation of using Bitcoin.

But this can also have the opposite effect due to the stature of Bitcoin in the world of cryptocurrencies. By exploring Bitcoin competitors, Bitcoin will benefit further from its status as the primary digital store of value. It will be a vindication that the underlying Bitcoin technology is transformative, and the community will be emboldened to protect it at all costs.

Bitcoin has unique characteristics that make it hard for any government to demolish. While government actions can limit its usage or adoption, it is virtually impossible for the government to completely demolish Bitcoin.
