To verify your 2-factor authentication using Google, follow these steps: 1. **Open Authenticator App:** Open the Google Authenticator app on your mobile device. If you haven't already, download and install the app from your device's app store. 2. **Scan QR Code:** On the website or platform where you're setting up 2-factor authentication, you'll typically find a QR code. In the Authenticator app, tap the "+" or "Add" button, then choose "Scan barcode." Point your device's camera at the QR code to scan it. 3. **Enter Verification Code:** Once scanned, the Authenticator app will generate a 6-digit verification code that changes every 30 seconds. Enter this code on the website or platform where you're enabling 2-factor authentication. 4. **Complete Setup:** After entering the verification code, the website or platform will usually prompt you to complete the setup process. This may involve confirming the code you entered or providing a backup code in case you lose access to your device. 5. **Backup Codes:** It's important to save the backup codes provided by the website or platform during setup. These codes can be used if you lose access to your device or the Authenticator app. Keep these codes in a secure place. Remember that 2-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring both your password and a verification code from your device. Make sure to keep your device secure and consider using additional backup methods in case your device is lost or inaccessible.
