An investment firm is a business entity that manages and invests funds from clients to achieve financial goals and generate returns. They offer a range of services, including portfolio management, investment advice, and financial planning.

Investment firms generate revenue through management fees (charged on assets under management) and performance-based fees (a percentage of client profits). This incentivizes them to achieve higher returns.

Choose an investment firm by evaluating their performance history, investment approach, fees, available services, and how well they match your financial objectives and risk tolerance. Research and recommendations can aid in your decision.

Active investing involves trying to outperform the market by selecting individual investments. Passive investing aims to match the market's performance by investing in index funds or ETFs.
